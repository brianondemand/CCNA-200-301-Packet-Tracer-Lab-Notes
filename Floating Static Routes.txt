A Floating Static Route is a backup route that only appears in the routing table when the primary route fails.

It is a static route with a higher Administrative Distance (AD) than your dynamic routing protocol. Because the number is higher, the router ignores it unless the primary route disappears.

	Primary Route (OSPF AD 110): Your main highway to work.

	Floating Static Route (AD 111): A dirt road you programmed into your GPS just in case.


The default Administrative Distance (AD) of a normal static route is 1.
